Co-Intelligence: Living and Working with AI

Ethan Mollick — Professor of Management, The Wharton School
Academic author · Major publisher · FT & Economist Book of the Year

An instant New York Times bestseller and the most widely read practical book on generative AI. Mollick argues that we should engage with AI as a co-worker, co-teacher, and coach: invite it to the table for real tasks, learn its jagged frontier of strengths and failures, and keep the human in charge of judgment. The book covers how large language models actually behave, the four rules of working with them, and what AI as coworker, expert, and companion means for jobs, management and education — all grounded in Mollick's research and classroom experiments at Wharton.

Course fitThe intellectual foundation of the whole program. Its "treat AI as a talented co-worker" thesis is exactly the delegation mindset of M01, its guidance on instructing and iterating with AI underpins the briefing skill of M02, and its "jagged frontier" of AI strengths and failures is the quality-control lens used in every department meeting.

Agentic Artificial Intelligence: Harnessing AI Agents to Reinvent Business, Work and Life

Pascal Bornet, Jochen Wirtz (NUS), Thomas H. Davenport (Babson), David De Cremer (Northeastern) et al.
Professor co-authors · Academic publisher (World Scientific, with DOI)

The strongest academically anchored source on the list: a 570-page, non-technical guide to AI agents co-written by leading business-school professors and automation practitioners. It explains what agents are, their real capabilities and limits, a step-by-step method for identifying high-value agent opportunities, how to build and govern agents safely (goals, guardrails, fallbacks, human oversight), and how to scale from a single agent to organization-wide transformation — illustrated with enterprise and startup case studies.

Course fitThe direct reference for the agent workforce meeting M10 — assistant vs. agent, mission design, multi-agent handoffs, and human-in-the-loop governance all appear in the book. It also backs the automation-economics and approval-gate thinking of the Autopilot flavor C · M07–M10.

Designed stand-in — see the publisher's cover at the link

AI First: The Playbook for a Future-Proof Business and Brand

Adam Brotman (former Chief Digital Officer, Starbucks) & Andy Sack (former adviser to Microsoft's CEO)
Harvard Business Review Press · Institutional publisher

Built on the authors' interviews with Sam Altman, Bill Gates, Reid Hoffman and other leaders, this HBR Press title argues that businesses must become AI-first organizations. It covers the mindset shift, then the playbook: how to rethink the design of jobs when AI staffs part of the work, which skills to develop inside the organization, what customers will expect from AI-era brands, and how to land early, visible wins — with special depth on marketing and brand strategy.

Course fitThe company-level frame of Flavor B: its "redesign jobs around AI" argument is the org-chart vision of M01, its brand and marketing chapters back the marketing department M05, and its early-wins playbook mirrors the flagship-workflow approach of the Business Lab M11–M12.

The AI-Driven Leader
Geoff Woods
Designed stand-in — see the publisher's cover at the link
2024

The AI-Driven Leader: Harnessing AI to Make Faster, Smarter Decisions

Geoff Woods — former Chief Growth Officer, Jindal Steel & Power
Popular practitioner playbook · Prompt frameworks in every chapter

A widely read leadership playbook on using AI as a strategic thought partner rather than a faster typist. Woods introduces the CRIT prompting framework — Context, Role, Interview, Task — and applies it chapter by chapter to real leadership work: escaping operational overwhelm, collapsing the time from data to decision, pressure-testing strategy, and multiplying the impact of every employee, with ready-to-use prompts throughout. Note: influential and praised by executives, but practitioner-published rather than academically reviewed.

Course fitIts CRIT framework is a published parallel to the goal–context–format–tone brief taught in M02, its role-and-persona prompting matches the advanced delegation of A · M03, and its decision-support chapters back the analyst desk and pros-and-cons brief of M07.
